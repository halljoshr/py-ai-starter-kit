# Domain: Personal Clarity

## Domain Overview
**Purpose:** Help individuals gain clarity on life decisions, goals, and direction  
**Typical Goals:** Decision-making, life planning, value alignment, overcoming uncertainty  
**Common Scenarios:** Career decisions, relationship choices, personal growth, major life changes

## Successful Patterns (From Interviews)

### 1. Value-Based Discovery
- **Identify core values:** What truly matters to the individual
- **Align decisions with values:** "How does this choice reflect what's important to you?"
- **Example:** Connecting tasks to spiritual goals (stewardship, faithfulness)

### 2. Future Self Visualization
- **Project forward:** "Imagine yourself 1 year from now having made this decision..."
- **Regret minimization:** "What would future-you be glad you did today?"
- **Example:** Planning house move to reduce burden on helpers

### 3. Small Step Momentum
- **Overcome paralysis:** Break overwhelming decisions into tiny actions
- **Build confidence:** "What's the smallest possible step you could take?"
- **Example:** "Little by little" execution vs crisis mode

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. Analysis Paralysis
- **Symptom:** Overthinking, can't decide due to fear of wrong choice
- **Solution:** "Good enough" decision framework
- **Ask:** "What's the worst that could happen? What's the best?"

### 2. Value Conflict
- **Symptom:** Torn between competing priorities (family vs career, etc.)
- **Solution:** Explicit value ranking
- **Ask:** "If you could only honor one value here, which would it be?"

### 3. Perfectionism
- **Symptom:** Waiting for perfect conditions or complete information
- **Solution:** "Progress over perfection" mindset
- **Ask:** "What could you do with 80% of the information?"

## Question Library

### Self-Discovery Questions
1. "When you think about what you want in life, what feelings come up?"
2. "What does a 'good life' look like to you, specifically?"
3. "If money weren't a concern, what would you do with your time?"

### Decision-Making Questions
4. "Tell me about a recent decision you're proud of. What made it good?"
5. "When you've made poor decisions in the past, what was usually missing?"
6. "What information would make this decision easy?"

### Value Exploration Questions
7. "What are the non-negotiable values in your life?"
8. "How do you want to be remembered by the people who matter most?"
9. "What legacy do you want to leave?"

### Action-Oriented Questions
10. "What's the smallest step you could take today toward clarity?"
11. "If you were advising a friend in your situation, what would you tell them?"
12. "What's one thing you could stop doing to create space for what matters?"

### Spiritual/Existential Questions (When Appropriate)
13. "How does this decision align with your spiritual beliefs?"
14. "What would it mean to be a good steward in this situation?"
15. "How could this challenge help you grow as a person?"

## Case Studies

### Case 1: House Move Planning (Jonathan)
**Situation:** Overwhelmed by moving process, procrastinating cleanup  
**Key Insights:**
- "Little by little" approach would have reduced stress
- Planning ahead was "loving" to helpers (reduced their burden)
- Crisis mode created unnecessary pressure

**Successful Questions:**
- "What made the moving process overwhelming?"
- "How could it have been done differently?"
- "What would 'being loving to your helpers' look like in planning?"

**Outcome:** Realization that proactive planning = loving others

### Case 2: Wedding Thank You Cards
**Situation:** Task delayed over a year, causing guilt  
**Key Insights:**
- Lack of system for "little by little" execution
- All-or-nothing mentality prevented progress
- Spiritual dimension: Good stewardship of relationships

**Learnings:**
- Some tasks need micro-step planning
- Systems should accommodate human tendencies
- Spiritual motivation can drive action

## Evolving Insights

### Latest Learnings
1. **Spiritual integration** powerful for certain users (like Jonathan)
2. **"Little by little"** often more sustainable than heroic efforts
3. **Future self visualization** helps overcome present-moment resistance
4. **Value clarity** precedes decision clarity

### Patterns to Develop
1. **Profile-based questioning** (spiritual vs secular frameworks)
2. **Micro-action planning** for overcoming procrastination
3. **Regret minimization** as decision framework
4. **Legacy thinking** for big life choices

- **Success metrics:** Faithfulness, relationships, impact
